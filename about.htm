About Gonarezhou National Park

Overview

Gonarezhou National Park is one of Zimbabwe’s largest national parks and forms part of the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Conservation Area. The name Gonarezhou means The Place of Elephants.

Location

The park is located in the south eastern part of Zimbabwe near the borders of Mozambique and South Africa. It includes the Runde and Save river systems.

Wildlife

The park is home to thousands of elephants, lions, hippos, leopards, buffalo, giraffes, cheetahs and more than 400 species of birds.

Main Features

  • The famous Chilojo Cliffs
  • Large elephant herds
  • Runde and Save river valleys
  • Stunning untouched wilderness
  • Rich plant and bird life
